OUR ACHIEVEMENTS

In recent advancements, significant progress has been made in the development of radiation-resistant materials specifically designed for spacecraft, greatly enhancing their durability and operational lifespans in harsh space environments. These innovative materials offer increased protection against cosmic radiation, thereby reducing wear and extending the functional period of spacecraft during long-term missions. Additionally, a highly efficient energy conversion system has been created, capable of harnessing solar energy even in the low-light conditions of deep space. This breakthrough technology ensures a reliable power supply for space missions operating far from the Sun, addressing one of the critical challenges of deep-space exploration. Complementing these efforts, ten new technologies have been patented with the aim of improving the efficiency and sustainability of space habitats by 2035. These technologies are focused on optimizing resource utilization, energy efficiency, and structural integrity, paving the way for more sustainable long-term habitation in space. Furthermore, a groundbreaking collaboration has been established with international space agencies, enabling the sharing of research data on advanced materials for long-duration space missions. This partnership fosters global cooperation and accelerates the development of solutions to challenges faced in interplanetary exploration and settlement. Together, these initiatives mark a transformative leap in the field of space technology and exploration.
